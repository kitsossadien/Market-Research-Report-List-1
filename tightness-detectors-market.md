<p><p>Tightness detectors are critical for assessing the integrity of seals in various applications. The Hammering Method involves applying a precise force to a structure and analyzing the resulting vibrations to detect leaks or faults. In contrast, the Ultrasound method uses high-frequency sound waves to identify gas or liquid leaks by detecting sound patterns that indicate anomalies in tightness. Both methods provide efficient, non-invasive ways to ensure safety and performance in industrial processes, contributing to preventive maintenance and operational efficiency.</p></p>
