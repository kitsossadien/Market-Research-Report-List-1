<p><p>The hydrochloric acid market is primarily divided into two types: synthetic hydrochloric acid and by-product hydrochloric acid. Synthetic hydrochloric acid is produced through industrial processes, mainly by reacting hydrochloride with water, ensuring high purity for various applications. In contrast, by-product hydrochloric acid is generated as a secondary product in processes such as the production of organic compounds or during the chlorination of compounds. Both types serve essential roles across industries, including chemicals, food processing, and pharmaceuticals.</p></p>
