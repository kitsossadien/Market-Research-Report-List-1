<p><p>The aquatic animal vaccines market comprises two primary types: injectable vaccines and dip vaccines. Injectable vaccines are administered directly to fish or shrimp, offering targeted immunity and precise dosage control. They are often used for high-value species requiring thorough protection. Dip vaccines, on the other hand, involve immersing aquatic animals in a vaccine solution, promoting rapid uptake through the skin and gills. This method is efficient for large groups, enhancing immunization while minimizing stress and handling time.</p></p>
