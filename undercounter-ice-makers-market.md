<p><p>The undercounter ice makers market features two primary types: water-cooled and air-cooled ice makers. Water-cooled ice makers utilize a water supply for cooling, making them efficient in high-temperature environments, but they require a water hookup. In contrast, air-cooled ice makers use ambient air for cooling, offering easier installation and lower operational costs, though their efficiency can drop in warm surroundings. Both types cater to varying needs in commercial settings, emphasizing efficiency and space-saving design.</p></p>
