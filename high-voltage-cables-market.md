<p><p>High voltage cables are essential for transmitting electricity over long distances, and they are primarily categorized into two types: AC power cables and DC power cables. AC power cables are designed for alternating current transmission, commonly used in traditional power grids. They are suitable for various applications, including residential and industrial uses. DC power cables, on the other hand, facilitate direct current transmission, often employed in renewable energy systems like solar and wind, as well as in high-voltage direct current (HVDC) applications.</p></p>
