<p><p>Circulating fluid bed (CFB) boilers are classified into three market types based on their operational pressures and efficiencies: subcritical, supercritical, and ultra-supercritical. Subcritical CFB boilers operate below critical pressure, typically having lower efficiency and higher emissions. Supercritical CFB boilers function at pressures above the critical threshold, allowing for higher thermal efficiency and reduced emissions. Ultra-supercritical CFB boilers push efficiency even further by operating at even higher temperatures and pressures, maximizing energy output while minimizing environmental impact.</p></p>
